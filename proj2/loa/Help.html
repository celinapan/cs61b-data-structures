Lines of Action

Lines of Action (LOA) is a board game invented by Claude Soucie. It is played on an 8×8 board by two sides, Black and White. Each side has twelve pieces. The two players take turns, each moving a piece, and possibly capturing an opposing piece. Two pieces are connected if they are adjacent horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Information and rules found at https://inst.eecs.berkeley.edu/~cs61b/sp20/materials/proj/proj2/index.html


Rules

1. The black pieces are placed on the top and bottom row of the board, while the white pieces are placed on the left and right column of the board.

2. The players alternate moves, starting with Black.

3. A player to move must move one of its pieces. A move takes place in a straight line, exactly as many squares as there are pieces of either color anywhere along the line of action.

4. A player cannot jump over its own pieces.

5. A player cannot jump over the opponent’s pieces, but can capture them by landing on them.

6. The goal of a player is to turn all own pieces on the board into one contiguous region. The first player to do that wins.

7. If one player’s pieces are reduced by captures to a single piece, the game is a win for this player.

8. If the move limit is reached and neither side are contiguous, the game is a tie.


Controlling Play

Commands: Commands are whitespace-delimited.  Other trailing text on a line is ignored. Comment lines begin with # and are ignored.

  uv-xy     A move from square uv to square xy.  Here u and v are column

            designations (a-h) and v and y are row designations (1-8): 

  new       Stop game and return to initial position.

  seed N    Seed the random number with integer N.

  auto P    P is white or black; makes P into an AI.

  manual P  P is white or black; takes moves for P from terminal.

  set cr P N

            Put P ('white', 'black', or '-') into square cr, and set the

            next player to move to N ('white' or 'black').  Used to

            set up a position, not for play.

  dump      Display the board in standard format.

  quit      End program.
